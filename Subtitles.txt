A village, with no more than one hundred inhabitants, sat just beyond the cacophonous symphony of industry from the city of New Garron.|12
This small community found their livelihood in the fertile land beneath them–they were farmers–but the work was arduous. Their tools, intricate, yet not pristine, were loaned to them, and would not function with naturally-born arms or hands.|21
Brass and iron mechanical arms were required. These, too, were loaned to the farmers by the same manufacturer.|10
Their debts wouldn’t pay themselves. |5
Children at the age of 9 would get their arms replaced, and they would help their parents in the earthy fields.|10
